Preventing corruption requires a combination of strong laws, transparency, and public awareness. Governments must implement strict anti-corruption policies and ensure accountability. Independent organizations should monitor activities and report corruption cases. Therefore, strong systems are necessary to reduce corruption.

Education plays a key role in preventing corruption. People must understand their rights and responsibilities. Awareness campaigns can help citizens recognize and report corrupt practices. Technology can also reduce corruption by automating processes and reducing human involvement. Therefore, education and technology are important tools.

Citizens also have a responsibility to act ethically and refuse to participate in corruption. Collective efforts from individuals, governments, and organizations are needed. Therefore, preventing corruption requires cooperation at all levels of society.
