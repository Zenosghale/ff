Bribery is one of the most common forms of corruption and involves offering money, gifts, or favors in exchange for special treatment or illegal advantages. It can occur in both public and private sectors and often affects decision-making processes. For example, a person may offer money to a government official to get a job or avoid legal punishment. Therefore, bribery distorts fairness and justice in society.

Bribery reduces trust in institutions and creates inequality among people. Those who can afford to pay bribes gain advantages, while others are left behind. It also leads to poor-quality services, as decisions are not made based on merit or need. Therefore, bribery negatively impacts both individuals and society as a whole.

Strict laws and awareness programs are necessary to reduce bribery. Governments must ensure transparency in all processes and punish those involved in corrupt practices. Citizens should also refuse to participate in bribery. Therefore, eliminating bribery is essential for a fair system.
