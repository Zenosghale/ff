Conclusion

Corruption is a serious issue that affects the political, economic, and social systems of a country. It leads to unfair practices, inequality, and loss of trust in institutions. Corruption slows down development and prevents resources from being used effectively for public welfare. It also creates a gap between the rich and the poor, making society unjust. However, corruption can be reduced through strong laws, transparency, accountability, and public awareness. Education and ethical values play an important role in preventing corrupt practices. Governments, organizations, and citizens must work together to eliminate corruption from society. Therefore, controlling corruption is essential for building a fair, transparent, and progressive nation.
