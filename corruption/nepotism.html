Nepotism refers to favoritism shown to relatives or close friends, especially in giving jobs or opportunities. It is a form of corruption because it ignores merit and fairness. In many organizations, people are selected based on personal relationships rather than qualifications. Therefore, nepotism reduces efficiency and fairness.

Nepotism discourages talented individuals and creates dissatisfaction among employees. It affects productivity and growth of organizations. When unqualified people are given important roles, it can lead to poor performance. Therefore, nepotism harms both individuals and institutions.

To prevent nepotism, organizations must follow transparent recruitment processes. Selection should be based on skills and qualifications. Policies must be enforced to ensure fairness. Therefore, eliminating nepotism is important for equal opportunity.
