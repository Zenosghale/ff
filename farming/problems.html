Farming faces numerous challenges that affect productivity and farmers' livelihoods. One of the major problems is unpredictable weather conditions caused by climate change. Droughts, floods, and irregular rainfall can damage crops and reduce yields. Farmers who depend on natural rainfall are especially vulnerable. Therefore, climate change is a serious threat to farming.

Another significant issue is the lack of proper irrigation facilities. Many farmers do not have access to reliable water sources, making it difficult to grow crops throughout the year. Soil degradation and loss of fertility due to overuse of chemicals also reduce productivity. Additionally, farmers often face financial difficulties due to low market prices and high production costs. Therefore, economic challenges also affect farming.

Lack of access to modern technology and education further limits agricultural development. Farmers may not be aware of improved farming techniques. Poor infrastructure and transportation make it difficult to sell products in markets. Therefore, solving these problems is essential for improving farming conditions.
