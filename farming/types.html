Farming is practiced in different forms depending on geographical conditions, climate, and available resources. One of the most common types is subsistence farming, where farmers grow crops mainly for their own consumption. This type is widely practiced in rural areas of developing countries where modern technology is limited. It depends heavily on natural conditions such as rainfall and soil fertility. Therefore, subsistence farming ensures survival but does not generate much income.

Commercial farming, on the other hand, is practiced on a large scale with the aim of selling produce in the market. It involves the use of modern machinery, fertilizers, and advanced irrigation systems to increase productivity. Crops such as wheat, rice, and cotton are grown for profit. This type of farming contributes significantly to the economy and supports industries. Therefore, commercial farming plays a major role in economic growth.

Mixed farming combines both crop cultivation and livestock rearing, providing multiple sources of income for farmers. Organic farming avoids chemical fertilizers and pesticides, focusing instead on natural methods to maintain soil fertility. Intensive farming uses small land areas with high input to maximize output, while extensive farming uses large land areas with less input. Therefore, different farming types are used to meet diverse needs and conditions.
