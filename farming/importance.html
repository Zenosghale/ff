Farming is essential for human survival as it provides food for the growing global population. Without farming, it would be impossible to meet the basic needs of people. Crops such as rice, wheat, and maize are staple foods consumed daily. Livestock farming provides milk, meat, and other products. Therefore, farming is the foundation of food security.

In addition to food production, farming contributes to economic development by providing raw materials to industries. Crops like cotton are used in textile industries, while sugarcane is used in sugar production. Farming also creates employment opportunities, especially in rural areas where alternative jobs are limited. Therefore, farming supports both industries and livelihoods.

Farming also plays a role in maintaining ecological balance. Proper agricultural practices help conserve soil and water resources. Sustainable farming methods reduce environmental damage and promote biodiversity. Therefore, farming is important not only for the economy but also for environmental sustainability.
