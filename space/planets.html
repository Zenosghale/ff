Planets are large celestial bodies that revolve around stars and do not produce their own light. In our solar system, eight planets orbit the Sun, each with unique characteristics. Inner planets like Earth and Mars are rocky, while outer planets like Jupiter are gas giants. Planets differ in size, temperature, and atmosphere. These differences help scientists understand how the solar system formed. Therefore, planets are an important part of space study.

Earth is the only known planet that supports life due to its suitable conditions. Mars is being studied for possible life and future human settlement. Jupiter is the largest planet and has strong gravity. Saturn is known for its rings made of ice and rock. Uranus and Neptune are extremely cold and distant. Each planet provides valuable scientific information. Therefore, studying planets helps us understand the universe better.

Space missions and satellites are used to explore planets. These missions collect data about surface conditions and atmospheres. Scientists analyze this data to learn about planetary systems. Future missions may allow humans to live on other planets. Therefore, planets remain a key focus of space exploration.
