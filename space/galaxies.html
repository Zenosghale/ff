Galaxies are massive systems of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity. The Milky Way galaxy contains billions of stars, including our Sun. Galaxies come in different shapes such as spiral and elliptical. They are spread across the universe and are extremely large. Scientists study galaxies to understand the structure of the universe. Therefore, galaxies are essential in space research.

Each galaxy contains millions or billions of stars and planetary systems. Galaxies can collide and merge over time. These interactions shape the universe. Telescopes help scientists observe distant galaxies. Therefore, studying galaxies helps us understand cosmic evolution.

Galaxies provide insight into the origin and future of the universe. They are key to understanding large-scale cosmic structures. Therefore, galaxies are an important focus in astronomy.
