Human trafficking is caused by various social and economic factors. Poverty is one of the main causes, as people seek better opportunities and become vulnerable to exploitation. Lack of education also increases the risk, as individuals may not be aware of dangers. Therefore, poverty and illiteracy are key factors.

Unemployment and lack of job opportunities force people to migrate, making them targets for traffickers. Political instability and weak law enforcement also contribute to trafficking. Criminal networks take advantage of these situations. Therefore, multiple factors lead to trafficking.

Addressing these causes requires improving education, creating jobs, and strengthening laws. Awareness programs can also help prevent trafficking. Therefore, understanding causes is essential for prevention.
