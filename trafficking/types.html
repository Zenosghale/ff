There are different types of human trafficking, each involving exploitation. Labor trafficking involves forcing individuals to work under harsh conditions without proper payment. Sex trafficking involves exploitation for commercial purposes. Child trafficking includes illegal adoption and forced labor. Therefore, trafficking takes many forms.

Each type violates human rights and causes serious harm to victims. Victims are often controlled through threats and violence. Therefore, trafficking is a severe crime.

Strong laws and enforcement are needed to combat all types of trafficking. Awareness and education can also reduce risks. Therefore, prevention requires combined efforts.
