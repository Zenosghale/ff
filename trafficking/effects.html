Human trafficking has severe physical and psychological effects on victims. Victims often suffer from abuse, violence, and poor living conditions. They may experience trauma, fear, and loss of freedom. Therefore, trafficking has long-lasting impacts.

It also affects society by increasing crime and reducing economic productivity. Families and communities are negatively impacted. Therefore, trafficking is harmful at both individual and societal levels.

Rehabilitation and support are necessary to help victims recover. Governments and organizations must provide protection and assistance. Therefore, addressing effects is important.
