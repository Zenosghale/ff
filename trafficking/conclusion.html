Conclusion

Human trafficking is a serious violation of human rights that affects millions of people around the world. It is driven by factors such as poverty, lack of education, and weak law enforcement. Trafficking causes severe physical, emotional, and psychological harm to victims and negatively impacts society as a whole. Combating trafficking requires strong legal measures, international cooperation, and effective enforcement of laws. Awareness and education are essential to prevent people from becoming victims. Governments, organizations, and individuals must work together to address the root causes and support victims. Therefore, eliminating human trafficking is essential for creating a safe, just, and humane society.
